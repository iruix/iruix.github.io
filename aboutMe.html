About Me

My Programming Experience

My first programming language I got in touch with was Python. I had a couple of school projects and I took an extra course by Tartu University. I started off slow, programming as only a sunday hobby, but as I did more, I started enjoying it more. It was difficult at first, because I fell into "Tutorial Hell" where I looked at tutorials all day and did not practice it myself. What truly gave me the push I needed, was University. I started taking programming more seriously and started learning on my own. I took many different programming courses, started creating my own projects and fell in love with programming.

The language I currently feel the most confident in, is PHP. I am currently learning also Swift and Python, but I have taken a paid Java course and in school I've completed JavaScript, Python, PHP, Web Design, Design, Databases and a few more courses. This year I took an extra Web Frameworks course, because of my interest in development. I'm taking time off every day to focus on personal projects and development. My best projects were shown on the front page.

My Life

Early Life

I grew up in a small place, called Järva-Jaani and as far as I can remember, I've loved being and working around computers. Helping relatives, playing games and generally goofing around. When I was younger, I also was into sports and because I was a little awkward back then, it helped me push myself when I felt down.

School Life

I was a quick learner. I understood everything very quickly and used logical thinking to learn new things. At school I started getting a few more friends. My friends had different interests, so I used to help them with computers. When I had the possibility in school, I always took the route which involved computers. That helped me learn many more things about computers. When I was in Gymnasium, I started drifting away from computer games and was looking for something creative to fill the void. I took up photography, music production and coding, which are all areas with a creative outlet. I worked all summers and I finished Gymnasium with only 3 other boys in my class.

University Life

When I had the chance to apply to university, I only applied to Tallinn University. The two choices I had were English and Computer Science. I chose computer science and I do not regret this decision. One thing I did not know about University was that it requires a lot of independent work. It taught me that I need to work hard for the things I want. Gymnasium was relatively easy, so that was a huge leap for me. I started taking things into my own hands and I started creating my own projects. I started learning more, I started doing more and I want to continue this mentality going forward from this point
